L1: loss function for measuring error rates
PSNR: Peak Signal-to-Noise Ratio
	- how different two images are in terms of pixel values
	- compare two images pixel-by-pixel and measure how much "error" or "noise" there is
	- higher = better (typically 20-40+ dB for good results)

SSIM: Structural Similarity Index Measure
	- evaluates structural similarity between two images, considering luminance, contrast, and structure
	- range: 0-1, 1 = perfect similarity
	- higher = better (typically 0.8-1 for good results)

LPIPS: Learned Perceptual Image Patch Similarity
	- how different two images look to a deep learning model that imitates human vision
	- lower = better (typically 0.0-0.5 for good results)
	- often the most important metric because it correlates best with human judgement
